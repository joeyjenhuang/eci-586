OVERVIEW

The primary goal of the Unit 1 readings and discussion is to build a basic understanding of Learning Analytics as a discipline and as a community of education researchers and practitioners whose primary goal is to understand and improve student learning. The required and self-selected readings this week provide an introduction to Learning Analytics in practice, including defining characteristics, data sources, and methods used. A secondary goal of our readings and discussion in each unit is to help you start generating ideas for your final course project.

DISCUSSION

In lieu of the peer interaction and discussion of course materials that normally take place “in class”, you’ll be asked to log in this week and engage with other members of our learning community through the course discussion forum. To help guide our discussions, we will collectively address a set of guiding questions provided in each forum. You are also welcome to add your own topics or questions for the class to discuss.

With the exception of the Self-Selected resource, you are not required to post to every thread or address every question listed below, particularly if you feel others in the class have thoroughly addressed the topic or questions. Our primary goal for these discussions is to collectively build our understanding of this week’s topics through back-and-forth dialogue and avoid a “collective monologue” in which we see 20 variations of the same post.

Guiding Questions

Chapter 1: Introduction

Reflecting on the course text and your self-selected reading, answer one or more of the following questions:

  1. How is LA defined? What is your interpretation of this definition?

  2. What are some similarities and differences when compared to similar fields such as Educational Data Mining (EDM) or Data Science?

  3. What is a core characteristic or feature of learning analytics that stands out to you? 

  4. From where does does learning analytics take its historical roots?

  5. What are some of the most common uses of learning analytics?

  6. What questions do you still have about learning analytics?

Chapter 2: Data Used in Educational Data-Intensive Research

Reflecting on the course text and your self-selected reading, answer one or more of the following questions:

  1. Among the types of educational data used in data-intensive research, with which are you most/least familiar? Most/least interested in working with?

  2. How might these sources of data be used to better understand or improve the educational contexts in which you work or are interested in researching?

  3. What are challenges typically faced with these types of data? What challenges or opportunities do you anticipate for yourself?

  4. How might learning analytics be used to better understand a dataset or educational context of personal or professional interest to you?

  5. How is the data intensive workflow similar to or different than other data analysis processes you may have come across before?

  6. What questions do you still have about data used in data-intensive research like learning analytics?

Chapter 3: Methods Used in Educational Data-Intensive Research

Reflecting on the course text and your self-selected reading, answer one or more of the following questions:

  1. What exactly does it mean to prepare for collaborative, data-intensive research?

  2. What are some common techniques used to wrangle data add what do they entail?

  3. What exactly is meant by exploratory data analysis (EDA) and how why might researchers explore data as part of workflow?

  4. What are some common approaches used in data-intensive research to model data and what do they entail?

  5. What exactly is involved in the communicate step of the data-intensive workflow and what makes a “good” data product used to communicate findings?

  6. What are some of the challenges of working with big data and data-intensive research in particular?

  7. How, and to what extent, might education practitioners collaborate on these steps?

ASSESSMENT

Grading

Grading for this week is fairly lenient, provided that it’s clear from your posts that you’ve done the required readings. Readings and discussion for each unit are worth 6 points and judged based on quantity and quality of your posts.

In term of quantity (3 points), you’ll be expected to add at least 3 posts in addition to an overview of your Selected Resource. Your initial post should be shared by Friday to help facilitate discussion.

In terms of quality (3 points), your posts over the next week should provide new or insightful contributions to the division questions or topics. There is no requisite for the length of each posting, and I strongly recommend taking a look at Table 1 of Gao’s productive online discussion model (summarized below) for ways to contribute to the conversation.

At minimum, your collective posts should also help us interpret or elaborate on discussion topics, questions, or ideas others have shared by “making connection to the learning materials” and should reference at some point the required video, course text and your self-selected resource.

Productive Online Discussion Model

Disposition 1: Discuss to Comprehend

Actively engage in such cognitive processes as interpretation, elaboration, making connections to prior knowledge.

  • Interpreting or elaborating the ideas by making connection to the learning materials
  • Interpreting or elaborating the ideas by making connection to personal experience
  • Interpreting or elaborating the ideas by making connection to other ideas, sources, or references

Disposition 2: Discuss to Critique

Carefully examine other people’s views, and be sensitive and analytical to conflicting views.

  • Building or adding new insights or ideas to others’ posts
  • Challenging ideas in the texts
  • Challenging ideas in others’ posts

Disposition 3: Discuss to Construct Knowledge

Actively negotiate meanings, and be ready to reconsider, refine and sometimes revise their thinking.

  • Comparing views from the texts or others’ posts
  • Facilitating thinking and discussions by raising questions
  • Refining and revising one’s own view based on the texts or others’ posts

Disposition 4: Discuss to Share Improved Understanding

Actively synthesize knowledge and explicitly express improved understanding based on a review of previous discussions.

  • Summarizing personal learning experiences of online discussions
  • Synthesizing content of discussion
  • Generating new topics based on a review of previous discussions
