OVERVIEW

In Unit 4 we learn about social network analysis as a both a theory and a method and look at specific examples of its use in educational contexts. The required and self-selected readings for this week provide an introduction to SNA in the field of education, including the historical, theoretical, and analytical foundations of a social network perspective. Our primary goal this week is to build a basic understanding of SNA as an approach to understand and improve student learning and the contexts in which learning occurs.

READINGS

For Unit 4, you will read Chapter 1 from Social Network Analysis and Education (Carolan 2014) and locate one additional research article, presentation, or informal study to help address our discussion questions for the week.

SNA and Education Part I: Theory and Concepts

The following chapter illustrates how social network analysis provides educational researchers with a different way of thinking about and measuring an learning processes and outcomes by that explicitly account for the importance of one’s relationships with others, as well as the larger pattern in which these relationships are embedded.

Guiding Questions

The Social Network Perspective and Educational Research Introduction

  1. Consider a network of which you are a part.
  • Who are the network’s actors?
  • What ties connect these actors?
  • Are there any groups within this network?
  • What relations within this network might be of interest to a researcher?
  • Finally, what makes this collection of actors a social network?
  1. Draft a research question that would be of interest to educational researchers that would require the collection of relational data.
  • What relational data would need to be collected?
  • For what reason would relational data need to be collected in order to address this question?
  • Using the same question from the previous response, explain the analytical level at which these data would need to be collected and analyzed.

ASSESSMENT

Grading

Grading for this week is fairly lenient, provided that it’s clear from your posts that you’ve done the required readings. Readings and discussion for each unit are worth 6 points and judged based on quantity and quality of your posts.

In term of quantity (3 points), you’ll be expected to add at least 3 posts in addition to an overview of your Selected Resource. Your initial post should be shared by Friday to help facilitate discussion.

In terms of quality (3 points), your posts over the next week should provide new or insightful contributions to the division questions or topics. There is no requisite for the length of each posting, and I strongly recommend taking a look at Table 1 of Gao’s productive online discussion model (summarized below) for ways to contribute to the conversation.

At minimum, your collective posts should also help us interpret or elaborate on discussion topics, questions, or ideas others have shared by “making connection to the learning materials” and should reference at some point the required video, course text and your self-selected resource.

Productive Online Discussion Model

Disposition 1: Discuss to Comprehend

Actively engage in such cognitive processes as interpretation, elaboration, making connections to prior knowledge.

  • Interpreting or elaborating the ideas by making connection to the learning materials
  • Interpreting or elaborating the ideas by making connection to personal experience
  • Interpreting or elaborating the ideas by making connection to other ideas, sources, or references

Disposition 2: Discuss to Critique

Carefully examine other people’s views, and be sensitive and analytical to conflicting views.

  • Building or adding new insights or ideas to others’ posts
  • Challenging ideas in the texts
  • Challenging ideas in others’ posts

Disposition 3: Discuss to Construct Knowledge

Actively negotiate meanings, and be ready to reconsider, refine and sometimes revise their thinking.

  • Comparing views from the texts or others’ posts
  • Facilitating thinking and discussions by raising questions
  • Refining and revising one’s own view based on the texts or others’ posts

Disposition 4: Discuss to Share Improved Understanding

Actively synthesize knowledge and explicitly express improved understanding based on a review of previous discussions.

  • Summarizing personal learning experiences of online discussions
  • Synthesizing content of discussion
  • Generating new topics based on a review of previous discussions
