Final Project: Independent Analysis Planning

ECI 586 Intro to Learning Analytics

Author

Dr. Shaun Kellogg

Published

September 22, 2024

Overview

Throughout this course, you conducted several analyses as part of the unit case studies and developed your first data product using Quarto in Unit 3. For your final course project, you’ll be asked to complete an independent analysis and create your own data product (e.g., slide show, dashboard, report, or mini “case study”) to showcase your analyses and findings. Your data product will bring together some of the data-intensive research workflow processes that we’ve learned this semester to help answer a research question you have about a dataset of your own choosing.

Assignment

Week 14 provides some space to plan and conceptualize your final course project. More details about the actual content and format of your data product are provided in Week 15, but in general, you will be expected to demonstrate you ability to prepare for analysis, wrangle and explore data, and communicate useful findings.

To help plan for your final project, your assignment this week is to:

  • create a forum post that addresses the questions below; and,

  • provide constructive feedback on proposed case studies for at least two of your peers.

To assist you in addressing these questions, I’ve included some potential data sources you may be interested in using for your final project, as well as some example student data products from past courses.

Prepare

  1. Why are you interested in this data/context and what research question(s) are you hoping to answer with your analysis?

  2. Who would most benefit from your analysis and why? How might your target audience use this information to take action?

Wrangle

  1. What data cleaning steps do you anticipate taking to prepare your data for analysis and why are they necessary? 

  2. What other variables/features (e.g. student grades, schools, dates, tweets, chapter numbers, demographics, etc.) might you need to include to address your research questions?

Explore & Model (Analysis)

  1. What metrics (e.g., percents, counts, p-values, sentiment values, degree, density, etc.) will you calculate and examine? And at what stage of your analysis?

  2. What tables or charts (e.g. text networks, word clouds, etc?) might you include to help readers understand and interpret your findings?

Communicate

  1. What key insights do you anticipate finding as a result of your analysis? Why would they be useful and what actions might they result in by your target audience?

  2. What ethical/legal issues, if any, need to be taken into consideration?

Assessment

This assignment accounts for one-fourth of your final project grade (6 points total). Responses that adequately addresses the questions above are worth a 1/2 point each (4 points total). In addition, you’ll be expected to provide constructive feedback to two of your peers. Each response is worth 1 point each (2 points total).
