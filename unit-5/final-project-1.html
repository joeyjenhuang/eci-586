Final Project: Independent Analysis Planning

ECI 586 Intro to Learning Analytics

Author

Dr. Shaun Kellogg

Published

November 20, 2023

Overview

Throughout this course, we’ve developed several data products as part of our tutorials and completed several case studies modeled after the Data-Intensive Research Workflow from Learning Analytics Goes to School (Krumm et al., 2018). For your final course project, you’ll be asked to complete an independent analysis and create your own data product (e.g., slide show, dashboard, report, or mini “case study”) to showcase your analyses and findings. Your data product will bring together some of the workflow processes we’ve learned this semester to help answer a research question you have about a dataset of your own choosing.

Assignment

Week 13, provides some space to plan and conceptualize your final course project. More details about the actual content and format of your data product are provided in Week 14, but in general, you will be expected to demonstrate you ability to prepare for analysis, wrangle and explore data, and communicate useful findings.

To help plan for your final project, your assignment this week is to:

  • create a forum post that addresses the questions below; and,

  • provide constructive feedback on proposed case studies for at least two of your peers.

Prepare

  1. What prior research, if any, inspired your case study and what data source(s), will you be using for your analysis?

  2. Why are you interested in this data/context and what research question(s) are you hoping to answer with your analysis?

  3. Who would most benefit from your analysis and why? How might your target audience use this information to take action?

Wrangle

  1. What data cleaning steps do you anticipate taking to prepare your data for analysis and why are they necessary? 

  2. What other variables/features (e.g. student grades, schools, dates, tweets, chapter numbers, demographics, etc.) might you need to include to address your research questions?

Explore & Model (Analysis)

  1. What metrics (e.g., percents, counts, p-values, sentiment values, degree, density, etc.) will you calculate and examine? And at what stage of your analysis?

  2. What tables or charts (e.g. text networks, word clouds, etc?) might you include to help readers understand and interpret your findings?

  3. While not required, what modeling techniques (e.g., logistic regression, random forests, sentiment analysis, community detection, etc.), if any, will you use to analyze your data and how do you anticipate these techniques will help you address your research questions?

Communicate

  1. What key insights do you anticipate finding as a result of your analysis? Why would they be useful and what actions might they result in by your target audience?

  2. What ethical/legal issues, if any, need to be taken into consideration?

Assessment

This assignment accounts for half of your final project grade (12 points planning, 12 points data product). Responses that adequately addresses the questions above are worth 1 point each (10 points total). In addition you’ll be expected to provide constructive feedback to two of your peers. Each response is worth 1 point each (2 points total).
